Why Digtal Storytelling?

        During the past six years, I took several theoretical courses in algorithms, complexity theory, and programming languages, as well as practical courses in database design, operating systems, mobile design, and software engineering methodology. When I review my projects, they are practical, stable, and effective, but all of them seem to fall short in their designs. I believe that I have accumulated a lot of programming experiences, but I may be lack designing skills. After learning how a project works behind scenes, I would like to gain more knowledge on the aesthetic aspect of the program and its usability. In addition, I believe that storytelling is a big part of a modern approach to website/app design. A design without a story is lifeless and unattractive. In contrast, incorporating stories in the design can bring the design to life by attracting users visually and encouraging participation and communication, which I would like to learn more. Personally, I love traveling and capturing the remarkable moments. My ideal part-time job is to travel around the world and post travel diaries providing inspiration to people.

Profolio Summary

         In my portfolio section, I placed screenshots of two websites I’ve built, along with some motion graphics. Those motion graphics are everyday life photos. I picked my favorite ones. The first website is called cat rescue. It was my first React.js project. It’s purely a design project; there is no backend, but I am going to add more functions later on. I used React.js as a frame, css to add styles, and sass to preprocess css. The purpose of building this site is to provide information to people who are interested in adopting/volunteering/sponsoring cats. Users can simply fill out information online, and then go to the shelter if they get approved. The site is designed to encourage people to help homeless cats. It’s also mobile-friendly.

         Another website is called ice cream shop. It uses asp.net and is a dynamic website for online customers ordering ice cream. Users can register and login to order their favorites for delivery. Customers can retrieve addresses or orders when they login. All information is saved to a database. Users are able to reset their password and receive an email with a random password. Various functionalities include: mobile-first design, multiple language support, and tutorials for the site. Techniques used in this project: Used asp.net to build the interface, design a relational model database, and create the table structure in MySQL. Created responsive interfaces using HTML5, CSS, CSS3 and Bootstrap (Grid System, button, navigation etc.). Developed dynamic interfaces with JavaScript (such as validating data, event handling, object visibility and client interactivity, etc.). Used JQuery for selectors, event functions, effects, and modifying HTML. Used plug-ins for features such as navigation drawers, CAPTCHA verification, slides for the homepage, and zoom-in-out for pictures.
